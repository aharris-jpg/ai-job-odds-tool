Key Findings

What stands out most when occupations are ranked by Vegas Insider’s modeled AI replacement odds.

Computer and office-based jobs dominate the top of the ranking.

Vegas Insider’s ranking shows Computer Programmers at 45% modeled odds, followed by Customer Service Representatives at 42% and Data Entry Keyers at 40%.

Administrative and documentation-heavy work appears especially exposed.

Medical Records Specialists carry 40% modeled odds, while Medical Transcriptionists sit at 38%, showing how strongly AI overlaps with structured information work.

Marketing, research, and communications roles also rank surprisingly high.

Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists show 39% modeled odds, while Public Relations Specialists reach 27%.

Healthcare splits into two very different risk profiles.

Medical Records Specialists rank at 40% modeled odds, while Registered Nurses sit near 4%, highlighting the divide between documentation-heavy and hands-on care roles.

Physical, manual, and in-person jobs dominate the safest end.

Many of the lowest-ranked roles cluster at 0% modeled odds, including Bartenders, Dishwashers, Electricians, and Firefighters.

High modeled odds do not mean a job disappears completely.

This tool estimates the odds AI replaces major parts of a role, not whether the occupation vanishes entirely.

How We Built This Tool

A journalist-friendly explanation of the methodology behind the ranking.

This tool begins with occupation-level AI exposure data from Anthropic, which estimates how much of each job overlaps with tasks AI can already perform or assist with.

Vegas Insider then transformed that source data into a modeled ranking system we call: “Odds AI Replaces Major Parts of Your Job.”

  1. Anthropic provides the source exposure score for each occupation.
  2. Vegas Insider uses that score as the base signal of AI task overlap.
  3. Vegas Insider converts that score into a probability-style metric for easier comparison across jobs.
  4. Vegas Insider ranks occupations from highest to lowest using that modeled odds figure.
This tool measures the odds AI replaces major parts of a job, not the odds a job disappears completely.

In practice, a higher score suggests AI could absorb a larger share of the tasks in a role. It does not guarantee unemployment or total occupation loss.
