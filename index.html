Key Findings

The broad patterns in the dataset are clear and consistent.

Digital desk jobs dominate the high-risk end.

Roles involving writing, coding, analysis, customer support, and admin work tend to show the highest modeled odds of AI replacement.

Physical and in-person jobs cluster at the safer end.

Jobs that rely on manual skill, on-site problem-solving, or real-world care generally rank much lower.

Our tool leads with modeled odds, not just exposure.

We transformed the source exposure score into a clearer probability-style number for comparison and ranking.

High odds do not guarantee job loss.

This tool estimates task takeover risk — not whether a job disappears completely.

How We Built This Tool

A simple explanation of the methodology behind the page.

This tool starts with occupation-level AI exposure data from Anthropic’s labor market research.

  1. Anthropic provides an AI exposure score for each job. That score reflects how much of the work in that occupation overlaps with tasks AI can already perform or assist with.
  2. We then convert that research score into our own modeled number: “Odds AI Replaces Major Parts of Your Job.”
  3. Jobs are ranked from highest to lowest using that modeled odds number, not the raw exposure score alone.
  4. The result is a simpler, more shareable way to compare occupations across the full dataset.

This tool estimates task takeover risk, not guaranteed unemployment or total job loss.

Source pipeline: Anthropic occupation data → processed dataset → modeled odds → rankings and search tool.
